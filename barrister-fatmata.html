Profile

Fatmata Sorie holds a Bachelor of Laws degree from the University of Sierra Leone and has 17 years post qualification experience. She is the Managing Partner of the firm. She also holds a Master degree (LL.M.) in International & Comparative Law from the George Washington University in the United States of America (a top 20 Law School in the United States) and is an Attorney in the State of New York. Her experience covers a wide range of legal issues which include litigation, conveyancing, mines and minerals law, corporate and commercial law, Administrative & Labour law, Family law and the law of Succession. She is currently listed in the world’s leading directory of lawyers; Chambers Global, wherein she is described as "very competent, straightforward, energetic and thorough. She is considered an up-and-comer in the Sierra Leone market. prominent and competent lawyer.”

She provides legal consultancy services to the Millennium Challenge Corporation Unit on a vast variety of legal issues including project closure support and general advisory services. She has provided legal support for an Energy Company, Jacobsen Elektro, for the installation of a 16MV thermal power plant in Freetown; a project jointly sponsored by the African Development Bank (ADB); Bank of Economic Development in Africa (BADEA); Saudi Fund and the Government of the Republic of Sierra Leone, prepared and reviewed documents and agreements for the British High Commission in Sierra Leone as well as prepared opinions for the British High Commission on international law issues relating to Eminent Domain and Sovereignty, researched Petroleum policies in Sierra Leone for Hebert Smith LLP, reviewed Advisory/Policy documents for international Companies, provided an opinion on transshipment in Sierra Leone and the taxation laws to a major Canadian Company, reviewed the MOU on Trade Relations between the United Kingdom and the Ministry of Trade of the Government Republic of Sierra Leone.

In addition to the above; she presented a Paper at a meeting convened by the British Chamber of Commerce in Sierra Leone on conflict of laws in Sierra Leone for International Investors and the UK Bribery Act. She currently commits part of her time to serving vulnerable women and girls in Sierra Leone serving as the current President of Legal Access through Women Yearning for Equality Rights and Social Justice (L.A.W.Y.E.R.S) - a professional organisation of female legal practitioners that has been in existence for 22 years .
