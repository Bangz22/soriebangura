Profile

Mr. Sorie is a Barrister & Solicitor and a Partner at Sorie & Bangura with 20 years post qualification experience. He is also an Attorney in the States of New York and Virginia in the United States. He is a skilled litigator and consummate solicitor who possesses a sharp intellect and is solution driven. Mr. Sorie is a result oriented professional, a performer who gets the job done while maintaining the highest ethical standard. He handles commercial and corporate matters including but not limited to incorporating companies, joint ventures, acquisitions, mergers, share transfers, liquidations, winding up and closeouts.

His experience also covers a wide range of legal issues which include litigation, procurement, conveyancing and real estate, mines and minerals law, family law, succession law including obtaining letters of administration and probate, and labour/employment law. He has had prior experience drafting legislation relating to energy, arbitration and agriculture and also has significant experience advising on contracts of employment, usually dealing with issues of termination, dismissals, benefits and redundancies.

Mr. Sorie routinely litigates civil matters in the superior courts of Sierra Leone including the Supreme Court (the Highest Court in Sierra Leone). He was a member of the Committee set up to revise the rules of procedure for the Fast Track Commercial Court, (a specialized commercial court established to ensure the speedy adjudication of commercial matters). He has represented companies such as Ericsson, Delmas and Dawnus, local NGOs (including the largest Farmer based organization in Sierra Leone – NaFFSL) as well as foreign NGOs (INGOs). He also has recovered debts for commercial banks such as the Sierra Leone Commercial Bank and Rokel Commercial Bank. He has recovered significant sums of money for clients including a $19 million judgment against one of the largest mining companies in Sierra Leone. He is retained solicitor for Orange Money Sierra Leone and his services have been utilized by the telecom giant Orange’s subsidiary in Sierra Leone.
